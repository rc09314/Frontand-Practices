A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

APJ Abdul Kalam Birth Anniversary: 10 Quotes from the Missile... The "first missile man of India" is Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, a name synonymous with India's missile development programs. He was instrumental in the development of the country's indigenous guided missiles, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles, and also contributed significantly to India's space program with the development of the first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). He went on to become the 11th President of India.

Biographies

  • Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a poor family.

  • Education: Graduated in physics from Madras University and later completed aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.

  • ISRO: Worked on the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle "Space Club".

  • Nuclear program: Played a key role in the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which cemented India's status as a nuclear.
